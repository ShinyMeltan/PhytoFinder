How to Use PhytoFinder

Select plants using the search bar above. When you click Find Common Chemicals, the system analyzes all selected plants and lists the chemicals they contain.

  • Chemical Name – The phytochemical found in one or more of your selected plants.
  • % of Selected Plants – How many of the plants you chose contain this chemical.
  • % of All Plants – How common this chemical is across the entire database of plants.
  • Score – A relative measure of how much more strongly this chemical appears in your selection compared to its overall prevalence. It is calculated as: (% in Selected Plants ÷ % in All Plants), rounded to the nearest whole number.

Use the Sort button in the top-right corner to change how results are ordered: by Score, Alphabetical order, % in Selected Plants, or % in All Plants.

About PhytoFinder

PhytoFinder was created as a personal project to explore the fascinating world of plants and the phytochemicals they contain. The idea came from wanting a simple, interactive way to connect traditional herbal knowledge with modern data sources, making it easier to identify patterns and discover potential remedies.

The website was designed and built by [Your Name Here] using modern web technologies (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) and is powered by the publicly available USDA phytochemical database. It started as an experiment to learn about coding, data handling, and plant science — and grew into a tool that others can use for research, curiosity, or inspiration.

The goal of PhytoFinder is not to provide medical advice, but to help highlight which chemicals appear in plants associated with certain uses. It’s meant as an educational and exploratory resource.

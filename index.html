How to Use PhytoFinder

Select plants using the search bar above. When you click Find Common Chemicals, the system analyzes all selected plants and lists the chemicals they contain.

  • Chemical Name – The phytochemical found in one or more of your selected plants.
  • % of Selected Plants – How many of the plants you chose contain this chemical.
  • % of All Plants – How common this chemical is across the entire database of plants.
  • Score – A relative measure of how much more strongly this chemical appears in your selection compared to its overall prevalence. It is calculated as: (% in Selected Plants ÷ % in All Plants), rounded to the nearest whole number.

Use the Sort button in the top-right corner to change how results are ordered: by Score, Alphabetical order, % in Selected Plants, or % in All Plants.

About PhytoFinder

PhytoFinder was created as a personal project inspired by Claire Kooy, a student at the University of South Florida studying Chemistry and Biology. She expressed frustration when trying to find the active chemicals in groups of plants that had similar medical effects. This led to the creation of a website that provides a simple and interactive way to compare the phytochemicals in plants, making it easier to identify patterns and discover potential remedies.

The website was designed and built by Aidan Wriggelsworth using GitHub and VS Code. It is written in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and powered by the publicly available USDA phytochemical database created by Dr. Duke and his team.

The goal of PhytoFinder is educational. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, or cure any disease, but to help highlight which chemicals appear in plants associated with certain uses. Always consult a healthcare professional before using plants or phytochemicals for medicinal purposes.
